Our Humble Beginnings

Manish and Sumith, driven by a passion for health and community well-being,were inspired to create a change.

In the quaint year of 2008, the seeds of Namma Shop were sown by the visionary minds of Manish and Sumith.

The genesis of this venture unfolded amidst the bustling streets, where a poignant realization struck the duo.

The local beverage market was marred by disheartening sights of rotting fruits, sugar-laden concoctions,

and the use of unfit water for making ice. Witnessing this predicament, Manish and Sumith felt a calling

to transform the prevailing narrative of unhealthy and exploitative practices in the beverage industry.

The FruitFul Vision

With a commitment to offering a healthier alternative, Namma Shop was established near Imagination College. The inspiration for Namma Shop stemmed from a desire to not only provide healthier alternatives to consumers but also to address the plight of local farmers who were grappling with the challenges of fair pricing for their produce. The decision to establish the shop adjacent to Imagination College was a strategic move aimed at catering to the needs of students who often found themselves caught in the hustle and bustle of college life, compromising on proper nutrition due to time constraints.

Community Roots

Over the years, Namma Shop became more than just a beverage destination; it became a community hub. Namma Shop commenced its humble journey with a modest menu featuring an assortment of fruit juices. The response from the students was immediate and enthusiastic. The demand for their offerings burgeoned, encouraging Manish and Sumith to expand their menu. This marked the evolution of Namma Shop from a humble juice stand into a comprehensive beverage and snack destination.

Continuing the Legacy

Today, Namma Shop stands tall as a testament to Manish and Sumith's dream, with multiple outlets across the city. he vision of Namma Shop was characterized by a commitment to sourcing the highest quality ingredients directly from local farmers. Fruits and nutrients, such as jaggery, were procured directly, fostering a fair trade practice that not only ensured freshness and quality but also supported the economic well-being of the local agricultural community.Sustainability was ingrained in the ethos of Namma Shop from its inception.

The usage of recycled cups underscored a conscious effort to minimize environmental impact.

Beyond being a purveyor of nutritious and sustainably sourced beverages, Namma Shop took

on the role of an educator, conducting training sessions on responsible citizenship for students and community members.
